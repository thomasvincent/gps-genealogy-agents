## Part III: The Costello & Henry Branch - The Gaelic Core (G6–G8)

### The Challenge: Irish Records in the Penal Laws Era

Tracing Irish Catholic families to G8 is the **"Hard Mode" of genealogy** because:
- **Catholic records destroyed**: Many parish registers burned or lost
- **Penal Laws** (1691–1829): Catholics banned from owning land, voting, holding office
- **Landlord-tenant system**: Irish families were tenants, not owners (harder to trace)
- **Lack of civil registration**: No government birth/marriage/death records until 1864

**Key sources**:
- **Tithe Applotment Books** (1823–1838) - tax records showing land occupiers
- **Griffith's Valuation** (1847–1864) - property valuation survey
- **Catholic parish registers** (where extant) - baptisms, marriages, burials
