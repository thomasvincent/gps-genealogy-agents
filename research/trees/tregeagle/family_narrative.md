# The Tregeagle Family
## Cornwall → New Zealand → American West (1837-1922)

## Primary Sources Confirmed

### James Tregeagle & Rosina Ann Oliver (James)

**Marriage:**
- Date: July 20, 1862
- Location: Kenwyn, Cornwall, England
- Groom: James Tregeagle (b. c. 1837, Cornwall)
- Bride: Rosina Ann Oliver James

**Etymology:**
- Village name "Tregeagle"
- Cornish: *Tre* = "homestead/settlement"
- Traditional interpretation: "town of gaggling geese"
- Alternative: *Gegal* = "throat/choking"
- Common family legend supports the "geese" interpretation

### Emigration to New Zealand

**Journey:**
- Ship: *Waitangi*
- Arrival: 1876
- Destination: Canterbury, New Zealand

**Settlement:**
- Relocated to Auckland, New Zealand

### Death of James Tregeagle

**Details:**
- Date: December 28, 1880
- Location: Auckland, New Zealand
- Age: c. 43 years
- Cause: Fell from barn (family account)
- Left widow: Rosina Ann with 9 children
