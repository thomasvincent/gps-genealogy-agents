**RE: Genealogical Research Request - Karl G. Maeser Memorial Building Construction Records (1909-1911)**

Dear Special Collections Staff,

I am conducting genealogical research on my ancestor, **James Eli Tregeagle** (1863-1934), who served as the lead stonemason and masonry contractor for the **Karl G. Maeser Memorial Building** at BYU during its construction from 1909-1911.

James Eli operated the **Belmont Stone Company** in Provo and was responsible for the exterior stonework on the Maeser Building, including the hand-carved Ionic columns, dentil molding, and Sanpete White Oolitic Limestone ashlar walls. The building is now listed on the National Register of Historic Places (NRHP #71000854).

I am seeking any archival materials that may document his work, including but not limited to:

### Primary Documents Requested

**1. Construction Records (1909-1911)**
- Building Committee meeting minutes or progress reports
- Contractor payment records or payroll logs mentioning "James Eli Tregeagle," "J.E. Tregeagle," "Belmont Stone Company," or "Tregeagle & Co."
- Correspondence between the university and masonry contractors
- Invoices or billing records for stonework materials or labor

**2. Architectural Documentation**
- Original blueprints or construction drawings by Ware & Treganza (the architectural firm)
- Any notations on plans specifying the stone contractor
- Specification documents listing materials and craftsmen
- Technical reports on the Sanpete limestone quarrying or selection

**3. Photographic Collections**
- Construction photographs from 1909-1911 showing stonework in progress
- Images of workers or craftsmen at the building site
- Any photos labeled with contractor names
- Dedication day photographs (October 9, 1911) that may include builders

**4. University Administrative Records**
- Trustee meeting minutes discussing the Maeser Building project
- Presidential correspondence regarding construction contracts
- Annual reports mentioning building progress or contractors

**5. Published or Archival Histories**
- BYU campus history collections referencing the Maeser Building
- Oral histories from Provo community members mentioning the Tregeagle family
- Historical society materials donated to BYU collections

### Specific Questions I Hope to Answer

1. **Contract Documentation**: Is there a formal contract or bid proposal from the Belmont Stone Company for the Maeser Building stonework?

2. **Payment Records**: Are there records showing the total amount paid to James Eli Tregeagle/Belmont Stone Company for the project?

3. **Construction Timeline**: Do records specify when the stonework began and ended, and whether there were any delays or complications?

4. **Material Sourcing**: Is there documentation of the limestone quarrying near Manti/Ephraim, Utah, and the transport to Provo?

5. **Craftsmen**: Did James Eli employ a crew for this project, and if so, are any workers' names recorded?

6. **Building Specifications**: Were there specific technical requirements for the stonework that would demonstrate the level of skill required?

7. **Quality Inspections**: Are there any contemporary assessments or reports on the quality of the stonework?

8. **Photographic Evidence**: Are there any construction-phase photographs showing James Eli Tregeagle or his workers on site?
