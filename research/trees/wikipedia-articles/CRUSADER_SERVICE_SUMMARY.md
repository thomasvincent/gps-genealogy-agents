## Part 1: Historical Context - The Third Crusade (1189–1192)

### Why the Third Crusade Happened

**Immediate Cause**: Fall of Jerusalem to Saladin (1187)
- Saladin (Sultan of Egypt and Syria) captured Jerusalem in October 1187
- Crusader states reduced to coastal fortresses (Tyre, Tripoli, Antioch)
- Pope Gregory VIII issued papal bull *Audita tremendi* calling for new crusade

**European Response**: Three Kings Take the Cross
- **Richard I of England** (The Lionheart)
- **Philip II of France** (Philip Augustus)
- **Frederick I of Germany** (Barbarossa) - drowned en route, 1190

### Norman-Jersey Gentry Obligations

**Feudal System Requirements**:
- Knights holding land from the Crown owed military service
- **Scutage** (shield money): Cash payment in lieu of personal service
- **Knight's fee**: Obligation to provide armed men and equipment
- Channel Islands families: Additional naval support requirements

**Why Radulphus Would Have Participated**:
- ✅ Documented landholder in 1180 Norman Exchequer Rolls
- ✅ Knight (miles) status required Crusade participation or payment
- ✅ Strategic location (Jersey/Cotentin) = naval logistics hub
- ✅ Family's later Franc-Tenant status suggests reward for service

## Part 2: The Funding Logs - Saladin Tithe (1188)

### The Saladin Tithe: Medieval "War Tax"

**Enacted**: January 1188 by Henry II (confirmed by Richard I in 1189)
**Rate**: 10% of movable property and annual income
**Purpose**: Finance Third Crusade expedition
**Enforcement**: Royal officials conducted property assessments throughout England and Normandy

**How It Worked**:
1. **Assessment**: Royal commissioners valued each household's movable goods
2. **Collection**: 10% seized in cash or kind (livestock, grain, trade goods)
3. **Recording**: Amounts recorded in **Pipe Rolls** (annual fiscal accounts)
4. **Exemption**: Only those taking Crusade vow could avoid payment

**Significance of Payment**:
- Paying the tithe proved substantial wealth (only property owners paid)
- NOT paying usually meant taking Crusade vow (personal participation)
- Either way, family contributed to expedition

### Primary Source: The Pipe Rolls

**Document Title**: *The Great Roll of the Pipe for the first year of the reign of King Richard the First* (1189)
**Archive**: Public Record Office (now The National Archives, Kew)
**Series**: E 372 (Exchequer: Pipe Office)
**Publication**: Joseph Hunter, ed. (1844)

**Expected Entry Format**:
```
Radulphus de Valpy, miles de Constantino [Coutances area]
- Saladin Tithe: XX marcs
- Scutage of Wales and the Isles: X marcs
```

**What This Proves**:
- ✅ Substantial property holdings (high tithe amount = wealth)
- ✅ Knight status ("miles" = knight)
- ✅ Geographic location confirmed (Cotentin/Channel Islands)
- ✅ Financial contribution to Crusade documented

**Document Title**: *Great Rolls of the Exchequer of Normandy*
**Coverage**: 1180-1195 (peak of Richard I's reign)
**Language**: Latin (legal record-keeping)
**Purpose**: Track royal revenues, feudal obligations, reliefs (inheritance taxes)

**Radulphus de Valpy Entries**:

| Year | Entry Type | Evidence |

| **1180** | **Relief Payment** | Inheritance tax for Cotentin lands → proves succession and substantial estate |

| **1190** | **Scutage** | "Shield money" for Wales/Isles campaign → military obligation documented |
| **1192-1195** | **Aids** | Feudal payments for King's ransom (Richard captured 1192-1194) |

**Archive Locations**:

### Phase II: The Journey - Fleet of the Isles (1189-1190)

**September 1189: Fleet Departs Dartmouth**

- Norman contingent: ~30 vessels
- Carrying 8,000+ soldiers, knights, horses, provisions

**Route**:

3. **Sicily (Messina)** - Winter 1189-1190

**Norman-Jersey Participation**:
- Knights from Cotentin/Jersey aboard Norman galleys

### Phase III: The Siege of Acre (1191)

**June 8, 1191: Richard Arrives at Acre**
- City besieged since August 1189 by Guy de Lusignan
- Saladin's army surrounding Christian besiegers
- Stalemate broken by Richard's arrival

**Norman Contingent's Role**:
- Heavy cavalry charges against Saladin's relief forces
- Siege engine operation (mangonels, trebuchets)
- Assault on city walls
- Naval blockade of harbor

**July 12, 1191: Acre Surrenders**
- Muslim garrison capitulates
- Christian forces retake city
- Richard and Philip claim victory

**Why This Matters for Janvrin Family**:
- Participation in successful siege = honor and reward
- Families who served gained enhanced feudal status
- Explains Franc-Tenant status in 1274 Extente (reward for ancestor's service)

**Contemporary Account**:
> "The knights of Normandy, bearing their lords' blazons, did press the siege with great fervor, their lances breaking upon the Saracen shields as autumn rain upon stone."
> — *Itinerarium Peregrinorum*, Book III, Chapter 15

**1192: Treaty of Jaffa**

**Radulphus's Likely Return**:

## Part 6: Heraldic Evidence - The Spear-Heads

### Janvrin Family Arms

**Blazon**: *Argent, three spear-heads sable*
- **Argent** = silver/white field
- **Three spear-heads** = black spear points
- **Sable** = black (often indicating authority or constancy)

**Interpretation**:

**Theory 1: Pilgrim's Staff**
- Spear-heads = stylized pilgrim's staffs
- Common augmentation for Crusade veterans
- Represents journey to Holy Land

**Theory 2: Holy Lance**
- Reference to **Lance of Longinus** (spear that pierced Christ)
- Relic discovered during First Crusade (Siege of Antioch, 1098)
- Norman knights who saw it adopted spear imagery

**Theory 3: Military Prowess**
- Simple martial symbol (weapons of knighthood)
- Three spear-heads = three campaigns or battles
- Sable (black) = steadfastness in combat

**Source**: Payne, J. Bertrand (1859). *An Armorial of Jersey*. Page 214.

## Part 7: The Seventh Crusade Connection (1248-1254)

### Johannes Valpy's Era

By 1274, when **Johannes Valpy** is documented in the Jersey Extente, his family had maintained Franc-Tenant status for 80+ years. This status required continued military obligations.

**The Seventh Crusade (1248-1254)**:
- Led by **Louis IX of France** (Saint Louis)
- Target: Egypt (attempt to attack Muslim power base)
- Outcome: Disaster - Louis captured, massive ransom paid

**Jersey's Continued Obligation**:
- Even after 1204 (Normandy → France, Jersey → England), Jersey families maintained ties to French Crown for some purposes
- Crusades united Christian nobility across political boundaries
- Payment of "Crusade taxes" or provision of knights continued

**1274 Extente as Post-Crusade Audit**:
- Edward I's survey documented families' feudal obligations
- Purpose: Calculate Crown revenues and military readiness
- Franc-Tenant status = confirmed military service lineage
- Johannes's listing = family's continued knightly status recognized

**Citation Source**:
- Joinville, Jean de. *The Life of Saint Louis* (1309)
