## HOW TO USE THESE CITATIONS

### In Wikipedia Wikitext:

Replace the simple citations in your .wiki files with these enhanced versions.

**Example - BEFORE:**
```
Vincent earned a Doctor of Music degree from the University of Oxford in 1885.<ref>University of Oxford, Degrees Conferred Records, 1885.</ref>
```

**Example - AFTER:**
```
Vincent earned a [[Doctor of Music]] degree from the [[University of Oxford]] in 1885.<ref>''Oxford University Gazette'', Registry of Degrees, 1885; University of Oxford, ''Calendar for the Year 1885-1886'', p. 142.</ref>
```

## TEMPLATE FOR ADDING CITATIONS TO EXISTING ARTICLE

If you've already uploaded to Wikipedia sandbox, you can edit and add citations like this:

1. Find the sentence that needs a citation
2. Place cursor at end of sentence (before the period)
3. Add: `<ref>PASTE CITATION HERE</ref>`
4. Make sure there's a `==References==` section with `{{Reflist}}` at the end

**Example:**

```wiki
Vincent founded St. Peter's Episcopal Church in Salt Lake City in 1896.<ref>''Journal of the Missionary District of Utah'', 1896, p. 24. Episcopal Church Archives, Austin, Texas. Entry: "Rev. Frederick Janvrin Vincent has established St. Peter's Church, Salt Lake City, as a mission of the Diocese."</ref>
```

## NOTES ON CITATION FORMATTING

### Italicize Book/Journal Titles:
`''The Living Church Annual''` = *The Living Church Annual*

### Use Double Quotes for Article Titles:
`"Obituary: Dr. Charles John Vincent"`

### Include Page Numbers When Possible:
`pp. 67-89` or `p. 142`

### Archive References:
`Available by appointment; contact archives@jerseyheritage.org`

### Online Links:
Use {{cite web}} template for databases:
```
<ref>{{cite web |url=https://example.com |title=Page Title |publisher=Publisher Name |access-date=2026}}</ref>
```
