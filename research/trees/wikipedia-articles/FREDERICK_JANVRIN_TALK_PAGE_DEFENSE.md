Frederick Janvrin held the title of '''[[seigneur]]''' within the unique legal system of the [[Bailiwick of Jersey]]. This feudal status places him within a specialized category of historical documentation recognized by Wikipedia under regional nobility guidelines.

* '''Historical context''': The '''Seigneurial system''' of Jersey persisted into the 19th century as a recognized form of land tenure under the [[Crown Dependencies|Crown]], unlike the abolished feudalism of mainland Britain. Janvrin's dual role as both feudal landholder and commercial merchant exemplifies the unique social structure of Channel Islands elite families.
* '''Legal significance''': Seigneurs held specific legal rights (land tenure, rents, court jurisdiction, colombier rights) that distinguished them from ordinary merchants.

'''Wikipedia precedent''': Wikipedia maintains articles on regional feudal office-holders and seigneurs when documented in published armorials and legal histories. See [[Seigneurial system of New France]] and articles on individual seigneurs in Canada and the Channel Islands.

* '''Historical impact''': The Newfoundland cod fishery was central to British Atlantic trade networks. Jersey merchants, including the Janvrins, dominated this trade due to their strategic geographic position.
