## üí° Pro Tip: Wikipedia Verification

When Wikipedia reviewers check your sources, they will:
1. Click on the links you provide (UCL LBS, National Archives)
2. Search for the subject's name in those databases
3. Verify that the source supports the claim

**Your advantage**: You've already done this verification before submission, so you know the sources work.

**Your disadvantage**: If a reviewer cannot easily verify a source (e.g., Jersey Heritage archive code), they may request additional proof or tag the statement.

**Solution**: For any non-online source, add a note in the citation:
```
<ref>Jersey Heritage Archive, L/C/40/B/2. Available by appointment; contact archives@jerseyheritage.org.</ref>
```

This shows the reviewer how they could verify the source if needed.
