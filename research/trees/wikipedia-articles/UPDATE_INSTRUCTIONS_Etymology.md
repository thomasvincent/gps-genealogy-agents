## Potential Reviewer Questions & Answers

### Q: "Are you sure the 12th-century lineage is accurate?"
**A**: The connection is documented in J. Bertrand Payne's *Armorial of Jersey* (1859), the authoritative reference on Jersey genealogy. The Société Jersiaise (Jersey's historical society) holds transcriptions of the medieval Extentes confirming the family's presence as franc-tenants.

### Q: "This seems like it's glorifying a slave owner."
**A**: The section provides historical context showing the family's 600-year presence in Jersey. This contextualizes Frederick Janvrin's activities within the broader transformation of gentry families from landholding to commerce. The slavery involvement is documented separately and not minimized.

### Q: "How does this medieval stuff relate to the 19th-century merchant?"
**A**: It demonstrates that Frederick Janvrin was not a "nouveau riche" merchant but a member of an established gentry family adapting to economic change. This is significant for understanding Channel Islands social and economic history.
