## Historical Context

### Irish Gold Rush Migration
- **1848-1855:** 100,000+ Irish arrive California
- **Nevada City:** Major Irish community
- **Occupations:** Mining, domestic service, labor
- **Religion:** Catholic (minority in Protestant California)
- **Community:** Close-knit, church-centered

### Nevada City/Grass Valley
- **Peak Gold Rush:** 1850s-1870s
- **Major Mines:** Empire Mine, North Star
- **Irish Population:** Significant (20-30% of miners)
- **St. Canice Parish:** Founded 1856, center of Irish community
- **Decline:** 1880s-1890s, mines playing out

### Fresno Agricultural Boom
- **1870s-1880s:** Railroad arrival
- **1880s-1900s:** Irrigation systems (raisin industry)
- **1900-1920:** Peak agricultural expansion
- **Irish Migration:** Many ex-miners moved to farm work
- **Thomas's Era:** Established agricultural center

### Colma Cemetery Culture
- **1900:** San Francisco bans new burials
- **Colma Founded:** 1924 (incorporated as "cemetery town")
- **Holy Cross:** Founded 1887 (San Francisco), moved to Colma
- **Irish Tradition:** Return to consecrated Catholic ground
- **Pattern:** Families maintained plots across generations
