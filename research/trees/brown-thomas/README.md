## The "California Story"

This family exemplifies the classic Irish-American California journey:
1. **Ireland → New Orleans** (typical immigration route)
2. **New Orleans → California** (Gold Rush opportunity)
3. **Nevada City mining** (1850s-1880s boom era)
4. **Dispersal to agriculture/urban centers** (mining decline)
5. **Maintained Irish-Catholic identity** (returned to Holy Cross Cemetery)

Thomas J. Brown's life (1860-1951) spanned from horse-drawn mining carts to post-WWII industrial boom, yet he returned to be buried with his mother and siblings in Colma.
