## Part III: The Longhurst Migration Pattern

### Surname Origins: Surrey, England

The **Longhurst** surname originates from:
- **Geographic origin**: Surrey, England (specifically the villages of Longhurst or Longhirst)
- **Etymology**: Old English "lang" (long) + "hyrst" (wooded hill)
- **First records**: 13th century Surrey land records

### The Ireland Connection: "The Pale" Theory

**Hypothesis**: The Longhurst family followed a three-stage migration:
1. **Stage 1** (1600s–1700s): Surrey → Dublin area ("The Pale")
2. **Stage 2** (1700s–early 1800s): Establishment in Ireland
3. **Stage 3** (1845–1860): Ireland → Ontario, Canada

**"The Pale"**: The area around Dublin under direct English control (1200s–1600s). Many English families settled here, maintaining English surnames and Protestant faith while living in Ireland.

**Why this matters**:
- Explains the "Anglo-Irish" designation (English surname, Irish residence)
- Suggests **Protestant** rather than Catholic (likely Church of Ireland/Anglican)
- Points to specific Irish records to search: Dublin/Leinster parish registers

### The Famine Migration (1845–1852)

The **Great Irish Famine** (1845–1852) caused massive emigration:
- **1 million deaths** in Ireland
- **1 million emigrants** (1845–1855)
- **Toronto** became a major destination (via ports of Quebec and Montreal)

**Longhurst arrival window**: 1845–1860
- Albert E. Longhurst born 1873 in Ontario
- His parents likely arrived 1845–1860
- Gravestone inscription may show Ireland birthplace

## Part IV: The Henry Ulster Connection

### Surname Distribution: Northern Ireland

The **Henry** surname in Ireland is concentrated in:
- **County Antrim** (highest concentration)
- **County Down**
- **County Londonderry** (Derry)
- **County Tyrone**

All of these counties are in **Ulster** (Northern Ireland).

### Scotch-Irish vs. Irish Catholic

**Two distinct "Irish" migrations to Canada**:

**1. Scotch-Irish (Ulster Scots)**:
- Originally Scottish Presbyterians who settled Ulster (1600s–1700s)
- Protestant (Presbyterian or Church of Ireland)
- Emigrated 1700s–1800s due to economic factors
- The **Henrys** likely fall into this category

**2. Irish Catholic**:
- Native Irish, Catholic faith
- Emigrated primarily during Famine (1845–1852)
- Often faced discrimination in Canada

**Why the distinction matters for research**:
- Different church records (Presbyterian vs. Catholic parish registers)
- Different emigration patterns (Scotch-Irish often via Belfast; Irish Catholic via Dublin/Cork)
- Different settlement patterns in Canada (Scotch-Irish often rural Ontario; Irish Catholic often urban centers)
