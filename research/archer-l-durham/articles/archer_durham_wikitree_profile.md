## Biography

=== Vital Information ===

'''Archer Lyman Durham''' was born on 9 June 1932 in Pasadena, Los Angeles County, California, United States.<ref name="census1940">1940 U.S. Census, Pasadena, Los Angeles County, California.</ref>

=== Parents ===

'''Father:''' Barney M. Durham (c. 1895–?), born in Georgia.<ref name="census1940"/>

'''Mother:''' Ruby Zenobia Sorrell (c. 1903–?), born in Vinita, Cherokee Nation, Indian Territory (present-day Oklahoma).<ref name="numident">Social Security Administration NUMIDENT records via FamilySearch.</ref>

=== Cherokee Freedmen Ancestry ===

Archer Durham descends from Cherokee Freedmen through his mother Ruby Sorrell. The Cherokee Freedmen were African Americans who had been enslaved by Cherokee citizens and who received Cherokee citizenship under the Treaty of 1866 following the American Civil War.

'''Maternal Grandparents:'''
* '''Morris A. Sorrell''' (also Maurice Sorrell), born 28 May 1874 in Indian Territory. Enrolled as Cherokee Freedmen (Card 193).<ref name="dawes193">Oklahoma Historical Society, Dawes Rolls, Cherokee Freedmen Card 193.</ref>
* '''Ida Manley''' (Dawes Roll #4136, Card #1515), enrolled as Cherokee Freedmen. Married Morris Sorrell on 5 October 1902.<ref name="dawes1515">Oklahoma Historical Society, Dawes Rolls, Cherokee Freedmen Card #1515.</ref>

'''Maternal Great-Grandmother:'''
* '''Kate Manley''' (Dawes Roll #4135, Card #1515), enrolled as Cherokee Freedmen. Her enrollment card notes she was "Transferred from Cherokee Freedmen Card D628."<ref name="dawes1515"/>

The Manley/Sorrell family resided near Vinita in present-day Craig County, Oklahoma.

=== Siblings of Ida Manley ===

According to Dawes Roll Card #1515, Ida Manley's siblings included:
* Frank Manley (Roll #4137)
* Sarah Manley (Roll #4138)
* Lela Manley (Roll #4139)
* Joseph Manley Jr. (Roll #4140)
* Willie Manley (Roll #4141)
* Daisy Manley (Roll #4142)

=== Military Career ===

Archer Durham graduated from high school in Pasadena, California, and entered the United States Air Force.

He rose to the rank of '''Major General''' (two-star general) during his Air Force career.<ref name="afbio">Official U.S. Air Force Biography, https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Biographies/Display/Article/107179/major-general-archer-l-durham/</ref>

'''Notable Assignments:'''
* Commander, 436th Military Airlift Wing, Dover Air Force Base, Delaware
* Director of Deployment, U.S. Transportation Command, MacDill Air Force Base, Florida

=== Legacy ===

The '''Maj. Gen. Archer L. Durham Award''' was established at Dover Air Force Base in his honor. The award recognizes individuals who demonstrate exceptional leadership in fostering teamwork and awareness of various cultures.<ref name="award">Dover AFB News, "Team Dover members win Maj. Gen. Archer L. Durham Awards," https://www.dover.af.mil/News/Article/762117/</ref>
